Why biodiesel?




1. It can reduce our dependence on foreign petroleum
Petroleum imports are
at record levels in the United States, and will continue to
rise as domestic oil supplies shrink. Our transportation sector, with its
great demand for gasoline and diesel fuel, relies almost exclusively on
petroleum for energy. Biodiesel can be produced domestically from
agricultural oils and from waste fats and oils. Because it can be
used directly in diesel engines, biodiesel offers the immediate potential to
reduce our demand for petroleum.

2. It can leverage limited supplies of fossil fuels
Regardless of whose perspective we choose to believe on the
future of coal, oil, and natural gas, their supply is, ultimately,
limited. Biodiesel can help us leverage our use of these fuels.

3. It can help reduce air pollution and related public health risks
