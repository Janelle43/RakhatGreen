Information

Biodiesel is a renewable diesel fuel substitute that can be made by chemically combining any natural oil or fat with an alcohol such as methanol or ethanol. Methanol has been the most commonly used alcohol in the commercial production of biodiesel. In Europe, biodiesel is widely available in both its neat form (100% biodiesel, also known as B100) and in blends with petroleum diesel. Most European biodiesel is made from rapeseed oil (a cousin of canola oil). In the United States, initial interest in producing and using biodiesel has focused on the use of soybean oil as the primary feedstock, mainly because this country is the worldís largest producer of soybean oil.

Life cycle analyses look at the whole picture of how a fuel is made, from ìcradle to grave.î The life cycles begin with the extraction of all raw materials to make petroleum diesel and biodiesel, and end with using the fuels in an urban bus.

Life cycle analysis

Life cycle analysis is a complex science. One characteristic of the process is that you have to choose very specific technologies and assumptions to represent very complex and diverse industries and systems. So life cycles can oversimplify ìreality.î

Scope

Life cycle analyses all have similar limitations. Incomplete data are the rule rather than the exception. We have varying degrees of confidence in the results, but the most reliable conclusions are for overall energy balance and CO2 emissions.
